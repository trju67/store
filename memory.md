# Memory

## Product Vision
- Build a curated luxury resale marketplace that feels editorial, polished, and easy to shop.
- Present fashion, watches, shoes, fragrances, accessories, and bundles with image-first hierarchy.
- Make the experience feel trustworthy, calm, premium, and modern rather than flashy or dense.
- Use client-provided photography for product and campaign imagery; avoid drawn or illustrated product stand-ins.

## Design Principles
- Prioritize clarity before decoration.
- Use strong hierarchy with generous spacing and restrained accents.
- Let imagery and typography do the heavy lifting.
- Keep interfaces smooth and refined, never busy.
- Favor fewer, better moments over many competing visual effects.

## UX Rules
- Shopping comes first: browsing, product discovery, and purchase actions must stay obvious.
- Every key action should be visible without hunting.
- Pages should scan quickly with minimal cognitive load.
- Motion should support orientation, focus, and delight without slowing the user down.
- Trust signals must be easy to find near purchase decisions.

## UI Rules
- Use clean layouts with open space and minimal chrome.
- Keep product cards light and image-led; avoid boxes inside boxes.
- Use subtle borders and shadows only when they clarify hierarchy.
- Avoid overcrowded grids and dashboard-like blocks.
- Keep controls tactile, obvious, and refined.

## Typography Choices
- Display font: Cormorant Garamond for editorial luxury headlines.
- Body font: Manrope for clean, modern readability.
- Headings should feel confident and airy, not compressed.
- Use restrained uppercase labels and generous letter spacing for metadata.

## Spacing System
- Base rhythm: 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128.
- Sections should breathe with large vertical spacing.
- Card interiors stay compact and disciplined.
- Product imagery should have more space than supporting copy.

## Color Palette
- Background: warm ivory and soft stone.
- Surface: translucent ivory and muted charcoal.
- Text: deep espresso / charcoal for comfort and contrast.
- Accent: muted champagne gold used sparingly.
- Secondary neutral: smoke gray for borders and metadata.
- No loud neon, candy tones, or flashy gradients.

## Animation Philosophy
- Motion must feel smooth, light, and premium.
- Use restrained fades, lifts, reveals, and parallax.
- Fast enough to feel responsive; slow enough to feel elegant.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Interaction Rules
- Hover states should confirm clickability immediately.
- Buttons should feel crisp with subtle lift, highlight, or depth.
- Product imagery may shift or scale slightly on hover.
- Detail-page imagery can tilt gently with cursor movement.
- Filters, quantity controls, and cart actions should have small tactile feedback.

## Cursor Rules
- Use a custom cursor layer only on pointer-capable devices.
- Keep it minimal, soft, and low-noise.
- Enlarge or label subtly over interactive elements.
- Never block clicks or obscure content.

## Hover Rules
- No exaggerated bounce or cartoon motion.
- Product cards: slight lift, image drift, refined shadow.
- Category cards: subtle scale, image motion, and label emphasis.
- Navigation: understated underline, opacity, or spacing response.
- Buttons: small translate/shine/depth response.

## Micro Interaction Rules
- Use subtle press states on buttons and controls.
- Thumbnail selection should animate cleanly and instantly.
- Add-to-cart should feel responsive and confident.
- Cart open/close transitions should be smooth and quick.
- Quantity changes should animate softly without layout jumps.

## Loader Behavior
- Show a fast first-load brand intro only once per session.
- Keep it elegant and brief with soft progress motion.
- Fade into the page without a harsh cut.

## Scroll Behavior
- Scrolling should feel smooth and premium but remain usable.
- Use subtle parallax only in hero and feature sections.
- Reveals should trigger naturally with minimal delay.
- Sticky navigation should stay light and non-intrusive.

## Things To Avoid
- No clutter.
- No 50 billion boxes in the middle of the page.
- No cheap generic ecommerce template look.
- No childish oversized border radiuses.
- No crowded dashboard feeling.
- No random flashy gradients.
- No visually noisy card overload.
- No bouncy or gimmicky animation.
- No tiny hard-to-hit controls.

## Component Style Rules
- Navbar should be thin, elegant, and easy to scan.
- Cards should rely on composition, spacing, and imagery over borders.
- Sections should have strong headings and editorial pacing.
- Product info panels should keep details grouped and readable.
- Trust messaging should feel premium, not corporate or heavy.

## Page-by-Page Goals
- Home: welcome users with clear brand positioning, curated categories, featured products, a signature editorial band, and trust cues.
- Collection: keep filtering simple, keep products image-first, and make scanning effortless.
- Product Detail: create a premium image-first layout with tactile gallery interaction and clear purchase confidence.
- Cart: make review and editing easy with clean summaries and obvious next actions.
- About: reinforce curation, authenticity, and the boutique luxury point of view.
